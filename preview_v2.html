Morning Memo
Three notes for the desk this morning

Iran-deal optimism reprices African oil exporters in 24 hours. Brent dropped 5.16% to $105.54 May 20 on US-Iran negotiating progress and satellite-confirmed supertanker transits through the Strait of Hormuz, blocked since March. Algeria, Libya, Nigeria, Angola, and Egypt (Suez transit volume) all face an inflection — prices remain ~50% above pre-conflict levels but the move down was steep. The Hormuz blockade had pushed Brent to $116 in March; full Persian Gulf flow recovery unlikely before late 2027 per ADNOC CEO.

ICC Libya hearing concludes today. Three-day confirmation hearing for Khaled Mohamed Ali El Hishri ends in The Hague — the first proceedings against a Libya warrant-bearer since the 2011 UN Security Council referral. Of fourteen public Libya arrest warrants, Hishri is the first to reach this stage. Whether judges send the case to trial will signal the post-Trump-administration trajectory of the Court's Africa caseload more than the merits of this case alone.

NGX retraces from 250,000 milestone. Lagos closed below the threshold for the first time in eight sessions after BUA Cement fell exactly 10% to ₦414. All-Share −1.02% to 249,062. YTD gain still 60.05% — Nigeria's currency-driven inflation rally remains the dominant 2026 story for African equities. Tomorrow's price action against 252,400 resistance reads the technical break either way.

Top Stories
3 leads · continent-wide
Sudan · Defections & UAE attribution
Second senior RSF commander defects in six weeks, names UAE as primary backer and alleges Darfur–Kordofan partition strategy
RSF commander Ali Rizqallah "Al-Savana" announced May 16 his intent to fight alongside SAF in Kordofan and southern Darfur. He named former Sudanese VP Hasabo Mohamed Abdel Rahman as the primary RSF-UAE coordinator and claimed Hemedti has lost operational control. His exit follows Gen. Adam's April 9 desert crossing through the Sudan-Egypt-Libya tri-border. The defections coincide with HFAC's 44–2 vote May 13 advancing the Jayapal–Jacobs resolution recognizing RSF genocide in Darfur and pushing for terrorist designation — Khartoum welcomed it May 14.
Sudan Tribune (May 14, 16) · The National (April 20) · House Foreign Affairs Committee
DRC · M23 + cobalt
M23 unilaterally withdraws from Ruzizi Plain; US firm signs tripartite cobalt agreement weeks after Treasury sanctions former president Kabila
Per ISW's May 15 Congo War Security Review, M23 withdrew from the Ruzizi Plain in South Kivu while continuing clashes with pro-government Wazalendo fighters in Rutshuru and the South Kivu highlands. A US-based firm signed an agreement with a Congolese state cobalt company and a Swiss commodities trader establishing a DRC-to-US supply chain — the first concrete output of the post-OFAC-Kabila track. Treasury designated former DRC President Joseph Kabila April 30 for AFC/M23 financial and political support.
ISW Critical Threats Project (May 15) · US Treasury (April 30) · BBC (May 1)
Libya · International Justice
ICC concludes Hishri confirmation hearing today — first Libya warrant-bearer to face the bench since 2011 referral
Three-day hearing May 19–21 examined evidence on crimes against humanity and war crimes tied to detention facility operations. Of fourteen public Libya warrants, Hishri is the first to advance. Per Human Rights Watch's Q&A briefing, the case is also the first proceedings before the ICC concerning Libyan atrocities since the UN Security Council referral.
AllAfrica (May 21) · ICC press · Human Rights Watch briefing
