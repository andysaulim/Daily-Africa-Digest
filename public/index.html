Morning Memo
Three notes for the desk this morning

**South Africa's inflation rate surged to a 20-month high of 4.0% year-on-year in April, up sharply from 3.1% in March**, driven primarily by fuel prices linked to the Iran war's disruption of global energy markets, Statistics South Africa reported. The data validates the CBN's assessment that Middle East hostilities are transmitting inflationary pressure across the continent. The DA is pushing to cut the Road Accident Fund levy component of fuel prices; the EFF has taken Finance Minister Godongwana to court over a fuel levy hike it calls a backdoor replacement for the failed VAT increase. (Daba Finance, May 21; DA, May 21; Scrolla, May 21)

**The Ebola Bundibugyo outbreak across the DRC and Uganda has reached 528 suspected cases and 132 deaths**, prompting WHO to declare a Public Health Emergency of International Concern. UNICEF flagged severe impacts on children; MONUSCO reaffirmed logistical support to responders. South Africa contributed $2.5 million to the Africa CDC–led response. Nigeria's NCDC confirmed zero domestic cases but activated preparedness protocols. WHO officials say insecurity in eastern DRC and population movement are complicating contact tracing. (Premium Times, May 21; SAnews, May 21; MONUSCO/ReliefWeb, May 21)

**Cluster munitions — banned under an international treaty — have been deployed for the first time in Mali's northern conflict by the Malian army and its Russian allies**, RFI reported. The escalation marks a dangerous new phase in a war pitting the junta against jihadist groups and separatist movements. No casualty figures were provided. Separately, the ISS documented how high youth unemployment in Kenya exposes young Africans to recruitment as mercenaries on Russia's frontline in Ukraine, underlining Moscow's expanding human-resource extraction from the continent. (RFI, May 21; ISS, May 21)
