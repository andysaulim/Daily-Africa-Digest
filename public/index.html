Morning Memo
1
The DRC-Uganda Ebola outbreak has reached 600 suspected cases and 139 deaths, with WHO warning a vaccine is nine months away. WHO's latest update, reported by BBC on May 21, records 600 suspected cases and 139 deaths across the DRC and Uganda from a rare Ebola species. UNICEF separately flagged acute impact on children across both countries, citing 528 suspected cases and 132 deaths in an earlier count (Premium Times, May 21). DRC canceled its FIFA World Cup training camp in response (BBC, May 21). MONUSCO has reaffirmed logistical support to the response. The nine-month vaccine timeline and conflict-affected geography make containment the central watch item.
2
JNIM jihadists enforcing a road blockade in Mali burned dozens of vehicles ahead of Eid, deepening fuel and food shortages. BBC reported May 21 that jihadist forces linked to JNIM enforced a blockade causing shortages of fuel and food ahead of next week's Eid holiday, with dozens of vehicles burnt. The blockade reflects JNIM's sustained capacity to project economic pressure across Mali's main road arteries, a pattern consistent with the group's graduated coercion strategy. Bamako's junta, under the Alliance of Sahel States framework, retains nominal control of the capital but has not publicly reported a military response. Watch for whether the blockade lifts before Eid or extends post-holiday.
3
A suspect in an alleged coup plot against President Tinubu has described plans to infiltrate the Presidential Villa using soldiers, SSS officers, and Julius Berger construction staff. Premium Times reported May 21 that coup suspect Umoru detailed a recruitment plan targeting soldiers, State Security Service officials, and Julius Berger employees to gain access to Nigeria's Presidential Villa. The disclosure, made in court proceedings, is the most operationally specific account to emerge from the alleged plot. Abuja has not confirmed the scope of the conspiracy. The case tests the Tinubu administration's security apparatus and could affect civil-military relations heading into the 2027 election cycle.
