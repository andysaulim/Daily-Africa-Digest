Morning Memo
1
Treasury sanctioned a Rwandan gold refinery for laundering Congolese conflict minerals. OFAC designated Gasabo Gold Refinery and an enabling network, alleging it smuggled at least 60kg of gold in early 2026, per the BBC and EIN News. The action lands amid contested US-brokered DRC-Rwanda diplomacy and aligns Washington with UN Group of Experts findings that Kigali profits from M23-held mining zones. Watch whether the designation chills the Doha and Washington peace tracks or sharpens Rwanda's denials.
2
DRC's Ebola response is losing contact tracing as nearly 300 confirmed cases vanish from health workers' radar. Africa CDC reports the whereabouts of almost 300 people who tested positive are unknown, with modelling forecasting thousands of deaths by September, per The Guardian and NYT. Officials warn of 'huge community transmission.' Morocco delivered nine tons of medical supplies, per Morocco World News. Watch case-count trajectory and whether cross-border spread reaches Goma-area displacement camps.
3
Kenya marked the second anniversary of the 2024 Gen Z protests under a security lockdown that detained 355 people. Police barricaded Parliament with barbed wire and choked Nairobi traffic; the CBD fell quiet as families laid flowers at the barricades, per BBC and Capital FM. Interior CS Murkomen called the day 'a testament to maturity' and reported no arrests in Nyanza, Coast and North Eastern. Watch for accountability demands over 2024 and 2025 protest deaths.
