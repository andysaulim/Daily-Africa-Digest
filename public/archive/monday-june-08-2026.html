Morning Memo
1
South Africa's xenophobia crisis escalates into a continental diplomatic event. President Ramaphosa on Jun 7 announced a five-pillar immigration crackdown — dedicated immigration courts, drone border surveillance, 10,000 new labor inspectors, biometric population register, and phased relocation of refugee reception centers to border posts (SAnews, Scrolla). Nigeria concluded screening of over 1,000 citizens and rescheduled evacuation flights (This Day). Malawi repatriated 150 nationals from Western Cape camps (Nyasa Times). Ghana received over 700 evacuees (Ghanaian Times). Watch: whether Pretoria's March and March vigilante movement forces further government concessions.
2
Nigerian troops overran Boko Haram's most fortified Mandara Mountains enclave and freed 360 captives. Joint Task Force North East (Operation Hadin Kai) breached a heavily defended position in southern Borno, rescuing hostages taken from multiple communities (This Day). Governor Zulum commended the operation (Vanguard). Separately, 42 Mussa schoolchildren abducted 25 days ago remain in captivity, with parents urging President Tinubu to accelerate rescue (Leadership). AFRICOM simultaneously reaffirmed its counterterrorism partnership with Nigeria (The Whistler). Watch: whether momentum from the Mandara raid extends to the Mussa hostage case.
3
UN Special Envoy Pekka Haavisto met TSC President Al-Burhan and General Kabbashi in Khartoum amid continued civilian toll. The meetings on Jun 7 mark Haavisto's latest push to restart mediation (SNA via AllAfrica). The same weekend, drone strikes on villages and a market in North Kordofan killed at least 15 civilians and wounded dozens; a separate strike hit an El Obeid fuel station (Dabanga). CIR and Sudan Witness satellite investigation exposed an arson campaign that splintered the symbolically important town of Kauda in the Nuba Mountains last month (Ayin Network). Watch: whether Haavisto secures any ceasefire commitment from either SAF or RSF.
