Morning Memo

South Africa's anti-migrant crisis has gone continental, drawing evacuations and diplomatic protests. Following June 30 protests by the March and March campaign, Home Affairs Minister Leon Schreiber said government processed over 500 buses to repatriate thousands of foreign nationals (SAnews, July 2). Uganda begins voluntary return of 265 citizens; over 100 Namibians and 74 Zimbabweans sought exit; foreign-owned shops were looted in Durban. Nigeria's Sola Enikanolaiye urged the AU to make xenophobia a top security priority. Watch AU peace-and-security response and coalition strain on Ramaphosa.

Amnesty's new report says RSF fighters deliberately targeted children and committed ethnic cleansing during the North Darfur assault, demanding an immediate ceasefire and international protection force (AI London/Dabanga, July 2). The UN Human Rights Council convenes an urgent debate July 3 on El Obeid, weighing a draft resolution mandating a fact-finding mission into war crimes and starvation as a weapon. Karnoi in North Darfur received 544 displaced families in three days. Watch the Geneva vote.

Sahel Monitor
AES · JNIM · ISSP · Africa Corps

AES juntas hold as civilian toll mounts in northern Mali: Mali, Burkina Faso and Niger remain under junta rule with no posture change across Bamako, Gao, Kidal, Ménaka, Ouagadougou and Niamey. Human Rights Watch reported the Malian army, allied militias and Islamist groups have all committed abuses with impunity since April's northern attacks, deepening the civilian toll (RFI, July 2).

Jihadist groups sustain abuses across shifting northern Mali alliances: HRW documented Islamist armed groups among perpetrators of abuses in northern Mali following the April assaults, with impunity entrenched as alliances shift (RFI, July 2). The pattern reinforces a widening security vacuum despite AES sovereignty claims. Watch displacement flows and any coordinated FUAES counter-operations.
