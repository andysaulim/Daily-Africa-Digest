Morning Memo
1
Jihadists struck Niger's main international airport, killing at least 35. Niger's Defense Ministry says 11 soldiers and 2 civilians died in the June 18 assault on Diori Hamani International Airport in Niamey, with the toll later rising to at least 35, per Agenzia Fides. The attack hit the capital of a junta-led state that expelled Western forces and leans on Russian security cooperation. It marks a rare strike on hardened capital infrastructure. Watch attribution claims and AES coordination.
2
Washington will phase out HIV funding for South Africa, home to the world's largest epidemic. The State Department, in an unsigned statement, said the US will end PEPFAR support for HIV prevention and treatment in South Africa, the NYT and BBC report. More than eight million South Africans live with HIV, the highest national caseload globally. The cut compounds the AGOA lapse straining Pretoria-Washington ties. Watch Treasury, PEPFAR transition timelines, and South African health-budget response.
3
DRC's Ebola outbreak is spreading and community resistance is worsening it. The WHO warns the Ituri-province outbreak is 'evolving fast,' AFP reports, as families stormed a quarantine centre to remove patients and a six-month-old girl was buried in Bunia. Health facilities have been attacked amid misinformation. Africa CDC's chief urged the continent to self-fund Ebola response and vaccines. Kenya intensified truck-driver screening. Watch case counts, vaccine deployment, and cross-border spread.
