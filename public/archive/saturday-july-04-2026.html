Washington cut critical funding for the AU's Somalia mission, forcing an emergency continental meeting. The African Union called an emergency meeting Friday after the US announced it would end funding over the government's lack of progress against al-Shabaab and its failure to take ownership of its own security, per Vanguard and Africanews. The cut threatens AUSSOM's viability at a moment of persistent insurgent pressure. The US withdrew troops from Nigeria after declaring a joint Islamic State operation a success. The drawdown lands alongside the Somalia funding cut, signaling a broader recalibration of the US security footprint in Africa. Watch whether Abuja and AFRICOM sustain intelligence cooperation, and how the exits square with AFRICOM's stated 'African-led, US-enabled' strategy.

The move deepens the juntas' sovereignty framing and rupture with Western-aligned institutions.
