Morning Memo
1
Kenya's High Court blocked the opening of a US-backed Ebola quarantine facility hours before it was set to go live. Katiba Institute filed the petition; the court issued conservatory orders barring any Ebola isolation facility tied to the US or any foreign entity (Capital FM, May 29). The planned 50-bed unit at Laikipia Air Base drew opposition from the Law Society of Kenya, the medical workers' union KMPDU (which issued a 48-hour strike notice), and former Deputy President Gachagua. Prime Cabinet Secretary Mudavadi and PS Oluga defended the arrangement as proactive. Secretary of State Rubio spoke with President Ruto, with the US committing KSh1.7 billion for Ebola preparedness (Capital FM, May 29). Watch: whether the government appeals or restructures the deal bilaterally.
2
Sudan's Al-Burhan and RSF's Hemedti used Eid al-Adha addresses to pitch sharply competing post-war visions. Al-Burhan praised SAF sacrifices and called for national dialogue; Hemedti's camp pushed its own political roadmap from El Geneina (Dabanga, May 29). Separately, a Sudanese civil-society coalition signed a new political charter in Nairobi titled 'Towards Building a New Nation,' seeking to bypass both belligerents (Dabanga, May 29). Sudan's intelligence chief General Mufaddal met Russian Duma Foreign Affairs Committee chair Leonid Slutsky in Moscow, signaling deepening SAF-Russia engagement (SNA, May 29). Watch: whether the dialogue call gains traction or remains rhetorical.
3
Ethiopia heads to the polls June 1 in an election shaped by Horn of Africa geopolitical rivalries. DW reports the seventh national election takes place as Gulf states compete for influence in the region and the Ethiopia-Egypt Nile dispute intensifies (DW via AllAfrica, May 29). Chatham House argues the vote alone cannot calm internal and regional conflict, citing ongoing instability in Amhara and Oromia. Ethiopia's water ministry reiterated its stance on 'prudent and reasonable use' of transboundary resources — a veiled reference to GERD (ENA, May 29). Watch: voter turnout in contested regions and any opposition boycott signals.
