Morning Memo
1
DRC's Ebola outbreak has crossed 900 suspected cases and 200 deaths, with crowd unrest at treatment centers complicating containment. WHO Director-General confirmed more than 900 suspected cases and 101 confirmed cases as of May 25; the DRC Ministry of Communication and Media reported deaths exceeding 200. Police fired shots in the air to disperse crowds attempting to reclaim bodies at a treatment center, signaling deepening community resistance. The Gates Foundation committed $15 million — $5 million each to Africa CDC, WHO AFRO, and frontline operations — for regional coordination and cross-border surveillance. WHO Africa Regional Director Prof. Mohamed Janabi warned that case counts will rise before the curve turns. Watch: Uganda and Liberia are on elevated alert; Liberia's House of Representatives has escalated legislative oversight of public health frameworks.
2
South Africa's xenophobia crisis is forcing an emergency cabinet meeting and triggering mass embassy registration by frightened Ghanaians. The Justice, Crime Prevention and Security Ministers convened an urgent meeting Monday to address anti-immigrant protests escalating across Durban and Johannesburg. Hundreds of Ghanaians queued at Pretoria's Ghanaian High Commission to register for emergency evacuation flights; Ghana's High Commissioner publicly accused protesters of xenophobia. Daily Maverick and Africa Check both flagged deliberate disinformation — including a recycled video falsely depicting foreign nationals stealing traffic lights — as an accelerant. A June 30 deadline circulating among agitators has refugees in active fear. Watch: local election proximity is incentivizing politicians to weaponize the issue, per Daily Maverick analysis.
3
Sudan's monetary system is fracturing along front lines, with RSF-aligned banking infrastructure emerging as a parallel financial order. A new Sudan Transparency and Policy Tracker report by Suliman Baldo documents how the RSF-aligned El Mustaqbal bank is partitioning Sudan's monetary system, deepening economic fragmentation beyond territorial division. The SAF-aligned government meanwhile reopened the Delling-Habila road linking North and South Kordofan after months of closure, allowing humanitarian organizations to resume medical supply transport. The UN separately called for a 'strong civilian front' to end the crisis. Watch: the monetary split — not just the military front — now constitutes a structural barrier to any post-conflict reunification scenario.

SENEGAL — POLITICS
Senegal's Parliament Speaker Resigns, Fueling Speculation Sonko Returns to National Stage
El Malick Ndiaye resigned as Speaker of Senegal's National Assembly, with observers speculating the move clears the path for ousted Prime Minister Ousmane Sonko to assume the post. The resignation deepens the political turbulence inside President Faye's governing coalition following Sonko's dismissal from the premiership. The speaker's position is constitutionally one step from the presidency under succession rules, lending the move outsized significance. Watch: any Sonko appointment would reorder the balance of power within Pastef and likely sharpen factional tensions.
