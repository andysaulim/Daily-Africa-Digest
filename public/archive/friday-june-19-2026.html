Morning Memo
1
JNIM struck Niger's main airport and an adjacent military airbase before dawn June 18, in the second such assault on Diori Hamani International this year. West Africa's al-Qaeda affiliate claimed the Niamey attack. Niger's government said the strike killed at least 11 soldiers and two civilians; the BBC reported 35 dead overall. Premium Times cited 11 soldiers and 22 attackers killed. The assault, hours of explosions and gunfire, follows a January strike on the same airport and underscores deepening insurgent reach into the capital. Watch AU and AES follow-up posture.
2
Zimbabwe's National Assembly passed the Constitution Amendment (No. 3) Bill, advancing a two-year extension of President Mnangagwa's term and the scrapping of direct presidential elections. New Zimbabwe reported the contentious CAB3 cleared the constitutionally required two-thirds majority. The BBC confirmed the bill would extend Mnangagwa's tenure by two years. The measure now moves toward final enactment, tightening executive control ahead of any 2028 transition. Watch Senate passage and constitutional challenges.
3
Renewed xenophobic violence in South Africa is driving thousands of Malawians and Zimbabweans toward home, straining consulates and repatriation shelters. GroundUp reported hundreds of Zimbabweans camped outside Cape Town's consulate and thousands of Malawians awaiting repatriation from Durban, where a second shelter will open. Daily Maverick reported Kwathema vigilante groups demanding immigrants leave by June 30. MTN's Ralph Mupita warned retaliatory boycotts of pan-African firms threaten jobs and integration. Watch SADC response and Zimbabwe's returnee rebate scheme.
