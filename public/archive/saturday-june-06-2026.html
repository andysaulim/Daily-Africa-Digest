Morning Memo
1
Fitch Ratings upgrades South Africa for the first time in over two decades. Fitch cited stronger fiscal discipline and lower-than-expected debt levels in its decision, per Africanews. The upgrade lands amid acute domestic pressure: xenophobic violence in Western Cape displaced migrants and prompted Nigeria to announce repatriation plans for over 1,000 nationals. Africa CDC and WHO launch a joint $518 million continental Ebola preparedness and response plan. The plan targets the ongoing Bundibugyo-species Ebola outbreak centered in DRC's Ituri Province, where 380 confirmed cases have been reported, per BBC. The U.S. CDC warned the outbreak could reach 20,000 cases absent strong public health measures, per Reuters. A clinic in Bunia is overwhelmed, per Africanews. Uganda-DRC border closures have left goods rotting at crossings, per Al Jazeera. Nigeria's NCDC Director-General Jide Idris acknowledged the country is not fully prepared but said containment capacity exists.

Separately, JNIM released a propaganda video of its Apr 25 attack on the Malian army camp and airport in Sévaré near Mopti, per Africanews.
