Morning Memo
1
Eastern DRC faces a converging Ebola, conflict, and hunger crisis that WHO calls catastrophic. WHO warned May 28 that a fast-spreading Bundibugyo-strain Ebola outbreak in Ituri is outpacing containment in a region battered by armed violence and displacement (UN News). Uganda temporarily closed its DRC border to contain cross-border transmission (Nile Post). Kenya's Law Society opposed a U.S. proposal to site an Ebola treatment facility in Nairobi, arguing it should be placed closer to the epicenter (Capital FM). WHO Director-General Tedros appealed directly to warring parties: 'Please, declare a ceasefire.' Watch for vaccination logistics and whether M23 fighting blocks health corridors.
2
Atiku Abubakar won the African Democratic Congress presidential ticket, reshaping Nigeria's 2027 race into a three-way contest. The former vice president polled 1,846,379 votes to defeat Chibuike Amaechi's 540,117 at the ADC convention (This Day). Atiku attacked the ruling APC's 'meaningless Renewed Hope' agenda and framed his candidacy as a national recovery mission (Vanguard). He will face incumbent Tinubu and Peter Obi. Watch whether ADC's organizational capacity can convert primary turnout into a general-election ground game across Nigeria's six geopolitical zones.
3
U.S. Senator Ted Cruz called for formal recognition of Somaliland, adding congressional weight to Hargeisa's bid. Cruz argued stronger ties with Somaliland would advance American security and strategic interests in the Horn (Horn Diplomat, May 28). Somaliland already hosts Israeli, UAE, and UK engagement. The statement lands as Galmudug political tensions mount over regional election sequencing in Somalia proper (Shabelle). Watch whether Cruz introduces standalone legislation or attaches language to an authorization bill — and whether Mogadishu escalates diplomatic pushback.

At least 16 students killed in Utumishi Girls Academy dormitory fire

Interior CS Murkomen assured a full investigation, noting police officers' children were among the victims (Capital FM). Wiper leader Kalonzo demanded urgent school safety reforms.
