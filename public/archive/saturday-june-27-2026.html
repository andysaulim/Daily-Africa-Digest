Morning Memo
1
Burkina Faso's junta severed diplomatic relations with France, the sharpest rupture yet in the Sahel's Western disengagement. Captain Ibrahim Traoré's government accused Paris of repeatedly acting against the country's interests, per Africanews and Reuters on June 27. France said it is weighing responses. The break extends Ouagadougou's pivot away from former colonial and Western partners, mirroring Bamako and Niamey. Watch whether Paris closes its embassy and how the move reshapes counter-JNIM cooperation across the AES bloc.
2
Kinshasa filed suit against Rwanda at the International Court of Justice, escalating the Great Lakes conflict into a legal arena. DRC accused Kigali of breaching conventions on genocide, racial discrimination and torture, BBC and Africanews reported June 26. The filing covers violations Kinshasa traces to the post-1994 period. It runs parallel to the stalled Luanda and Nairobi tracks and the M23 occupation of eastern cities. Watch Kigali's response and whether the case complicates US-brokered peace and minerals diplomacy.
3
OFAC sanctioned Rwanda's Gasabo Gold Refinery, accusing it of smuggling DRC minerals. The Treasury action alleges the refinery moved at least 60kg of gold in early 2026, per BBC and Discovery Alert on June 27, in coordination with EU conflict-gold measures. The move directly targets the financing channel UN experts link to M23. It lands as Washington courts DRC copper. Watch follow-on designations and whether Kigali contests the attribution.
