Morning Memo
1
AFRICOM confirmed it withdrew most US forces from Nigeria after concluding an operation against Islamic State. Premium Times reported the AFRICOM commander said "We have withdrawn much of our forces that were just there for that operation," while retaining an intelligence partnership. The Jerusalem Post and TheCable carried parallel accounts. The drawdown reshapes the US security footprint in West Africa's largest economy as Abuja fights ISWAP and bandit networks. Watch for Nigerian government reaction and whether intelligence-sharing scales up to offset the reduced presence.
2
Burkina Faso, Mali and Niger deposited formal notifications to withdraw from the ICC's Rome Statute. Amnesty International's West Africa director Marceau Sivieude warned the move "consecrates impunity" and threatens war-crimes victims' access to justice, per allAfrica. Human Rights Watch urged reversal. The coordinated exit fits the AES bloc's decolonial sovereignty framing while Western observers read deepening insulation from accountability. Watch AU and ECOWAS responses and whether other juntas follow the three Sahel states.
3
Anti-migrant violence spread across South Africa, killing at least three Ugandans and a Ghanaian national. The Nile Post reported three Ugandan deaths; Ghanaian Times confirmed the murder of 35-year-old Kwabena Boagen in Nyanga on June 29. SAPS made 900 arrests Tuesday for public violence and looting, per GroundUp. Uganda evacuated a first batch of 273 citizens; Nigeria funded repatriation flights. Ghana demanded justice. Watch diplomatic fallout and whether the July "March and March" mobilisations escalate further.
