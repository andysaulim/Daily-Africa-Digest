Morning Memo
1
South Africa's June 30 anti-migrant deadline triggered a regional displacement event. Citizen-led groups including March and March ordered undocumented foreigners out by Tuesday, prompting mass marches across Johannesburg, Durban, Cape Town and Pretoria. Government processed over 8,000 nationals for repatriation at Beitbridge in under two weeks; Zimbabwe repatriated 3,624 citizens by June 26 (Scrolla, New Zimbabwe, June 30). Ramaphosa urged calm; Amnesty condemned 'citizen's arrests.' Watch looting arrests and Malawi border bottlenecks at Beitbridge.
2
Sudan's army claimed dual battlefield gains in Darfur and Blue Nile. The SAF-aligned Joint Force said it captured the Chadian border town of Kolbus in West Darfur after RSF clashes, while Darfur governor Minni Minnawi claimed severe RSF losses (Dabanga, SUNA, June 29-30). Separately, Ayin Network detailed a covert UAE logistics network sustaining the RSF through eastern Libya. China cancelled roughly $50m in Sudanese debt. Watch El Fasher's siege status and Kordofan front movement.
3
DRC banned mass gatherings in Kinshasa and three provinces to contain an Ebola outbreak. The ban covers Kinshasa, Tshopo, Haut-Uele and Bas-Uele (allAfrica, June 30). Uganda's UPDF outlined border prevention measures along the DRC frontier, and WHO and Africa CDC launched a Continental Incident Management Support Team (ReliefWeb, June 30). The outbreak compounds eastern DRC's M23 conflict and displacement crisis. Watch case counts and regional livestock-trade restrictions.
