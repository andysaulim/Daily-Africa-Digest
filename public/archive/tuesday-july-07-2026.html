Morning Memo
1
Tuareg separatists and al-Qaeda affiliates seized Anéfis, tightening rebel control over northern Mali. South Africa's anti-migrant violence escalated into a regional diplomatic crisis as a third Malawian died during repatriation. Nyasa Times reports Pretoria issued condolences to Lilongwe over the death. Nigeria says two nationals were killed, one allegedly by police (BBC); Uganda confirmed three citizens dead (Nile Post). GroundUp filmed police assisting Limpopo residents rounding up roughly 200 immigrants. Zimbabwe's Mnangagwa appealed to Zulu and Xhosa traditional leaders and sought donations to repatriate stranded citizens. South Africa's Home Affairs Committee begins a two-day Musina oversight visit today.

Kenyan police locked down Nairobi ahead of Saba Saba protests, arresting demonstrators in plainclothes operations. Capital FM reports plainclothes officers detained youths near the Office of the President on Harambee Avenue as security agencies enforced heightened restrictions. Martha Karua accused the government of violating constitutional freedoms through roadblocks. CPJ demanded a probe into an attempted abduction of a journalist who criticized President Ruto. Mombasa and Nakuru stayed calm. Watch for casualty reporting and Ruto's response as commemorations continue.
