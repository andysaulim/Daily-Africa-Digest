SENEGAL
President Faye sacks PM Sonko and dissolves government amid deepening Pastef rift
Bassirou Diomaye Faye dismissed Prime Minister Ousmane Sonko on Friday and dissolved the entire cabinet after months of public and private tension over governance and party hierarchy (BBC, May 23). Sonko — Faye's former mentor who engineered the Pastef movement — had just hours earlier condemned Western 'tyranny' over homosexuality legislation, underscoring the ideological volatility (Africanews, May 22). Supporters massed outside Sonko's Dakar home, while Al Jazeera reports the rupture could stall IMF bailout talks critical to Senegal's fiscal stabilization. Watch for Sonko's next move — street mobilization or party breakaway — and the identity of any replacement PM.

EBOLA
WHO raises DRC risk to 'very high'; Uganda confirms five cases; mob burns Ituri treatment center
WHO declared the DRC Ebola risk 'very high' nationally and 'high' regionally as the Bundibugyo-strain outbreak spread through conflict zones in Ituri and North Kivu (BBC, May 23). A mob in Ituri torched a hospital after staff refused to release a suspected victim's body (NYT, May 22). Uganda confirmed three new cases — a health worker and the driver of the index patient — and suspended all cross-border traffic with DRC within 48 hours (Al Jazeera, May 23; Africanews, May 22). UK scientists are developing a Bundibugyo-specific vaccine that could reach trials within months (BBC, May 23).
Al Jazeera · May 23, 2026 ↗
NIGERIA
DHQ blames displaced Boko Haram fighters for coordinated Oyo school attacks; hostages still held
Nigeria's Defence Headquarters attributed last Friday's attacks on three schools in Oriire LGA of Oyo State to Jama'atu Ahlis-Sunna Lidda'Awati Wal-Jihad (JAS/Boko Haram) fighters pushed south from the northeast (Premium Times, May 22). Oyo State police denied social-media claims that hostages had been freed (Leadership, May 22). Burial rites for slain teacher Adesiyan Adegboye began Friday in Ogbomoso (Vanguard, May 22). The attribution marks a significant southwestward expansion of JAS operational reach — watch for DHQ troop redeployments and community self-defense mobilization calls already emerging from civil society groups (Leadership, May 22).
