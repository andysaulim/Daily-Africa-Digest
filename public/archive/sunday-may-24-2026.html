Ebola outruns contact tracing in eastern DRC as WHO raises outbreak risk to its highest level. The World Health Organization elevated DRC Ebola risk to its highest tier as Bloomberg reported flights halted and supplies running low in affected zones. 2
Senegal President Faye dissolves government and sacks Prime Minister Ousmane Sonko after months of internal tension. Faye fired Sonko on May 23, ending an uneasy cohabitation between the two Pastef founding figures who swept to power in 2024 on an anti-corruption platform. Dakar residents reacted with a mix of shock and resignation, with Sonko retaining a passionate youth following. AllAfrica framed the episode as the latest iteration of Africa's post-revolutionary governance trap: young reformers capturing power only to fracture on its exercise. The split raises questions about Pastef's legislative cohesion and Senegal's fiscal reform calendar heading into a critical IMF review cycle. (Africanews, May 23; AllAfrica, May 24)
3
China eliminates tariffs on African goods as Beijing maneuvers around Trump-era trade disruptions. The Wall Street Journal reported May 24 that China scrapped tariffs on African exports in a move explicitly framed as an effort to outmaneuver the Trump administration's trade posture. The timing coincides with AGOA's lapsed status and the absence of U.S. replacement trade legislation, leaving African exporters searching for alternative preferential access. The African framing — sovereignty leverage and BRICS+ pivot opening — maps directly onto this: Beijing is filling a structural gap Washington created. Watch whether specific commodity categories, particularly critical minerals, receive preferential treatment in implementation rules. (WSJ, May 24)

DRC / EBOLA
Ebola Outruns Containment as WHO Raises DRC Risk to Highest Level
The World Health Organization raised the DRC Ebola outbreak to its highest risk tier as Bloomberg reported flights halted and medical supplies depleted in eastern Congo. Contact tracing has collapsed in key zones, and Al Jazeera reported May 24 that attacks on Ebola centers are intensifying as communities resist burial protocols. Uganda confirmed three new cases and the Africa CDC placed 10 countries on alert. The U.S. response has been complicated by USAID cuts; the Washington Post noted May 23 that a 'weakened U.S. system' is meeting one of the most troubling outbreaks in years.

SENEGAL
Faye Fires Sonko, Fracturing Pastef's Post-Election Coalition
Senegalese President Bassirou Diomaye Faye dissolved the government and dismissed Prime Minister Ousmane Sonko on May 23 after months of reported tensions between the two Pastef co-founders. Sonko retains a large youth following in Dakar and beyond, making the split a potential electoral liability for Faye ahead of municipal cycles. AllAfrica framed the episode within a broader African pattern of revolutionary governments fracturing on the realities of governance. Watch whether Sonko forms an opposition vehicle or remains within the Pastef structure.

TRADE
China Zeros Tariffs on African Exports to Exploit U.S. Trade Gap
China eliminated tariffs on African goods in a move the Wall Street Journal characterized on May 24 as a direct effort to outmaneuver Trump-era U.S. trade policy. The action lands as AGOA remains lapsed with no replacement legislation advancing in Congress, leaving African exporters without preferential U.S. market access. Beijing's move reinforces the BRICS+ pivot framing dominant in African policy commentary. Critical minerals trade provisions in the implementation rules will determine how much strategic leverage China extracts from the concession.
