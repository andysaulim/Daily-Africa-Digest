Morning Memo
Three notes for the desk this morning

Mali's military and Russian allies deploy cluster munitions in the north for the first time. RFI reports cluster bombs — banned under the Convention on Cluster Munitions — were used by Malian armed forces and their Russian partners against jihadist and separatist positions in northern Mali. The escalation marks a qualitative shift in the decade-long conflict and will intensify pressure from rights groups and European capitals. Watch for AU and ECOWAS statements and whether the Bamako junta acknowledges or denies use.

South Africa's inflation surged to a 20-month high of 4.0% in April, driven by fuel costs linked to the Iran war. Stats SA data show CPI climbing from 3.1% in March, per Daba Finance. The DA is pushing to cut the Road Accident Fund levy embedded in fuel prices, while the EFF is challenging Finance Minister Godongwana's fuel levy hike in court. Putco bus fares rise 10% Jun 1. The inflation spike complicates the SARB's rate path and coalition fiscal politics ahead of Nov 4 local government elections.

Ebola case count hits 528 suspected infections and 132 deaths across DRC and Uganda, WHO warns. UNICEF and WHO flag insecurity and population movement in eastern DRC as complicating surveillance and contact tracing, per Premium Times. Nigeria's NCDC confirmed zero domestic cases but activated precautionary protocols. MONUSCO reaffirmed support to the DRC response. South Africa's government contributed $2.5 million to Africa CDC for the outbreak. Watch for PHEIC-triggered travel advisories and cross-border screening gaps.
