Mali's junta posts a $3.5 million bounty on JNIM leader Iyad Ag Ghaly, weeks after his forces launched a major April offensive. The Bamako military government announced the reward for information leading to the arrest or killing of Ag Ghaly, head of the Sahel branch of al-Qaeda, alongside smaller rewards targeting Tuareg separatists. RFI reported the bounty at €3 million; DW cited $3.5 million — a divergence analysts should flag. The move signals junta willingness to escalate counterterrorism posture independent of French or Western frameworks, consistent with the AES bloc's sovereignty-assertion pattern. Watch whether the bounty draws defectors from JNIM's Tuareg flank or hardens them. (DW, RFI · Jun 5, 2026)

Ramaphosa announces continental envoy deployment as anti-migrant violence forces thousands of foreign nationals toward departure. President Cyril Ramaphosa confirmed South Africa will dispatch envoys across Africa and globally to manage migration pressure, and is expected to address the nation on a comprehensive migration strategy. Daily Maverick reported migrants describing fear and uncertainty as xenophobic protests spread, with many stating they plan to leave. Nigeria separately announced plans to repatriate more than 1,000 nationals from South Africa. Ramaphosa, speaking alongside Kenyan President William Ruto in Midrand, rejected the xenophobia label and called for African-led economic solutions. Watch the Ramaphosa national address for policy specifics. (SAnews.gov.za, Daily Maverick, Vanguard · Jun 5, 2026)

AU and IGAD observer missions publish conflicting voter statistics following Ethiopia's June 1 general election. The African Union and IGAD both lauded Ethiopia's democratic progress in preliminary statements, but the Reporter (Addis Ababa) flagged that the two missions cited disparate voter turnout figures — a data divergence that complicates the electoral legitimacy picture. The EU delegation and Ethiopian civil society coalition CECOE separately reported high turnout and a largely peaceful process. National Election Board of Ethiopia announced a successful conclusion. Opposition access and counting transparency remain the primary watch items before final results are certified. (Reporter, ENA · Jun 5, 2026)

ETHIOPIA
AU and IGAD Diverge on Voter Figures After Ethiopia's 7th General Election
Ethiopia's National Election Board declared the June 1 vote successfully concluded, and civil society coalition CECOE reported high turnout and largely peaceful conduct, but the Reporter (Addis Ababa) found that AU and IGAD preliminary observer reports cited conflicting voter statistics. The statistical divergence between the AU and IGAD missions is the primary credibility risk: final certified results and a reconciliation of the turnout data will determine whether the election withstands opposition scrutiny.
