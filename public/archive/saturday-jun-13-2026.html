Morning Memo
1
The United States deported a group of migrants to the Central African Republic, including an Iranian pro-democracy activist and an Iranian woman, in a third-country removal operation that drew immediate legal challenge. A US deportation flight landed in CAR on Jun 13, carrying individuals who lawyers say have no connection to the country, according to Reuters and AP. The Iranian activist's attorney confirmed the removal. The operation extends the Trump administration's third-country removal framework — previously applied to El Salvador and Rwanda — to one of the world's most fragile states. CAR's absorption capacity and MINUSCA's presence make the destination legally and logistically contentious. Watch for congressional response and OFAC implications given CAR's active sanctions program.
2
Congolese security forces dispersed hundreds of protesters in Kinshasa on Jun 13 who rallied against President Félix Tshisekedi's push to change the constitution, injuring at least two opposition figures. Police broke up the demonstration after clashes with pro-government supporters, according to Reuters and Africanews. Critics argue the constitutional revision would allow Tshisekedi to extend his hold on power beyond term limits. The crackdown signals Kinshasa's low tolerance for organized opposition as the DRC simultaneously manages an active M23 conflict in the east, an Ebola outbreak in Bunia, and strained relations with Kigali. Watch whether opposition coalitions escalate street pressure or seek regional arbitration.
3
President Cyril Ramaphosa moved legally to halt the South African parliament's impeachment inquiry into the 'Farmgate' cash-in-sofa scandal, a maneuver that reframes the ANC's internal succession calculus ahead of coalition budget negotiations. Reuters and Bloomberg both reported the court challenge on Jun 13, citing Ramaphosa's legal team's argument that the parliamentary process is constitutionally flawed. The Farmgate scandal — involving undisclosed cash found at his Phala Phala farm — has shadowed Ramaphosa since 2022. A successful legal stay would buy time but intensify ANC internal pressure. South Africa's GNU coalition partners, including the DA, have signaled the scandal undermines governance credibility. Watch the Pretoria High Court's response timeline.
