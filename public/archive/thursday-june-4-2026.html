Morning Memo
1
Heavy fighting erupted across Mogadishu Wednesday night and continued Thursday, disrupting airport access and key roads as Somalia's political standoff turns kinetic. Forces aligned with President Hassan Sheikh Mohamud's government and opposition factions exchanged gunfire in what Shabelle and Horn Diplomat describe as the most dramatic escalation of a constitutional crisis that has paralyzed governance. IGAD issued an urgent call for immediate de-escalation, warning that continued violence threatens Somalia's stability and future (ENA). Watch for whether the planned opposition mass protest proceeds and whether AMISOM successor forces intervene.
2
An Abuja federal court sentenced four men to death by hanging for the June 2022 St. Francis Catholic Church massacre in Owo, Ondo State, which killed 43 worshippers. The court identified the defendants as members of an Al-Shabaab-linked terrorist group (Vanguard). The conviction arrives amid a fresh wave of insecurity: armed bandits killed at least 20 in Sokoto's Dangulbi community (Leadership), and the Oyo State school abduction saga deepened as police denied viral claims that kidnapped children had been rescued (Premium Times). Teachers staged protests in Zamfara, Yobe, and Akwa Ibom demanding protection.
3
Zimbabwe won a non-permanent UN Security Council seat with overwhelming General Assembly support, even as Parliament cut short debate on the controversial Constitution Amendment Bill No. 3 amid death threats to legislators. The UNSC win gives Harare a global platform it has lacked for years, but CAB3 — which would extend presidential powers — drew sharp domestic pushback. MPs cited security fears and refused to work late into the night (New Zimbabwe). Separately, Zimbabwe's government acknowledged for the first time that elections have been 'dogged by allegations of rigging,' eroding presidential legitimacy (New Zimbabwe). Police warned they would act against anyone disrupting the CAB3 process (263Chat).
