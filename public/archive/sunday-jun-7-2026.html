SOUTH AFRICA / MIGRATION
Vigilante Groups Issue June Ultimatum to Undocumented Migrants
Anti-migrant vigilante networks in South Africa are reportedly conducting door-to-door sweeps demanding undocumented foreign nationals leave the country by end of June, according to RFI reporting cited by AllAfrica. Post-apartheid specialist Cécile Perrot told RFI the violence reflects scapegoating linked to economic stress rather than organized political mobilization. The pattern mirrors earlier xenophobic waves in Johannesburg and Durban. Tunisia separately saw protests outside the UNHCR Tunis headquarters on Jun 7 demanding deportation of sub-Saharan African migrants, signaling a continent-wide hardening of anti-migrant sentiment.
