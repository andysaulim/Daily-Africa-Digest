The IMF publicly challenged Nigeria's planned $5 billion Total Return Swap with First Abu Dhabi Bank, warning the structure is opaque and complex. A criminal complaint seeking prosecution of RSF members for war crimes landed in a Nairobi court under universal jurisdiction. The filing, reported by Capital FM (Jun 9), represents the first known attempt to use Kenya's legal system against Sudanese belligerents. It arrives as Human Rights Watch separately called on global actors to reject impunity for defecting armed-group commanders (AllAfrica, Jun 10). Meanwhile, the Darfur Network for Human Rights documented 18 civilians killed in drone strikes across North Kordofan between Jun 6-8.

Global Witness published a detailed investigation tracing conflict coltan smuggled from eastern DRC through Rwanda into global electronics supply chains. The report challenges due-diligence systems designed to prevent conflict-mineral leakage (AllAfrica, Jun 10). It lands as the DRC continues to accuse Kigali of profiting from M23-controlled mining zones. The findings will intensify pressure on downstream buyers and could influence EU due-diligence regulation enforcement. Watch for Kigali's response and any corporate supply-chain audits.
