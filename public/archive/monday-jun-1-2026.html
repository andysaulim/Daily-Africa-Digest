Morning Memo
1
Ethiopia's seventh general election opened Monday with 143 polling stations failing to operate and voting interrupted in Oromia and Amhara. The National Election Board of Ethiopia (NEBE) confirmed the closures were security-related, per Addis Standard (Jun 1). Prime Minister Abiy Ahmed's Prosperity Party faces 42 competing parties but is widely expected to retain power. AU observer mission chief Uhuru Kenyatta briefed the African diplomatic corps in Addis Ababa ahead of the vote. RFI framed the election as 'overshadowed by war, instability and muted opposition'; state outlet ENA cast it as a democratic milestone. Watch: final turnout figures and whether NEBE certifies results in contested regions.
2
South Africa's Section 89 Impeachment Committee convened for the first time Monday, beginning its inquiry into President Cyril Ramaphosa over the Phala Phala scandal. Thirty-one MPs met to elect a chairperson and launch the new inquiry after a Constitutional Court ruling compelled parliament to restart the process, per SAnews.gov.za (Jun 1). ANC Secretary General Fikile Mbalula said the party will not use the process to shield Ramaphosa, per Scrolla (Jun 1). The committee's composition and timeline will determine whether proceedings extend past the ANC's 2027 election preparations. Watch: chairperson election outcome and first witness schedule.
3
Nigeria's Oyo State school abduction crisis deepened as a presidential delegation arrived in Ogbomoso and teachers' union called an indefinite strike. Chief of Staff Femi Gbajabiamila led the presidential delegation to Oyo following the May 15 abduction of pupils and teachers from three schools in Oriire Local Government Area, per Leadership (Jun 1). The Nigeria Union of Teachers ordered all Oyo primary and secondary school teachers to strike, per Leadership (Jun 1). Protesters gathered in Ibadan under the 'Bring Back Our Pupils and Teachers' banner demanding immediate release of all hostages. Watch: any ransom or operational update from security forces.
