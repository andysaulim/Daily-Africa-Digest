Morning Memo
1
A rare Bundibugyo Ebola strain has killed more than 500 in eastern DRC, WHO said Tuesday. The UN health agency reported the outbreak is still expanding as testing and treatment efforts accelerate, per UN News. The Bundibugyo species carries no licensed vaccine, complicating containment across Central and East Africa. CFR warns the spread tests weak local public-health systems and global health-security readiness. Watch case dispersion beyond the epicentre and whether cross-border movement into Uganda and Rwanda triggers regional alerts.
2
The UN Human Rights Council unanimously condemned RSF for escalating violence around El Obeid. Dabanga reported the Council warned North Kordofan's capital faces a growing risk of mass atrocities as the Tasees bloc backed a ceasefire. Separately, the Sudanese Armed Forces said air defences downed a hostile strategic drone over Al-Andaraba in North Kordofan, per SNA. El Obeid sits on RSF supply routes toward Darfur. Watch whether the Council condemnation shifts UAE-backing scrutiny and whether SAF holds the Kordofan line.
3
President Emmerson Mnangagwa signed Constitutional Amendment Bill No. 3, extending his second term. New Zimbabwe reported the law effectively lengthens Mnangagwa's tenure and rewards parliamentarians with an extra two years. The move sharpens ZANU-PF succession tensions and draws opposition charges of self-entrenchment. It lands as Harare says 78,112 nationals returned from South Africa since May amid xenophobia fears. Watch legal challenges and factional maneuvering within ZANU-PF ahead of the delayed transition.
