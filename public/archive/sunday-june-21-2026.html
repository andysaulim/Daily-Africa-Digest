Morning Memo
1
INEC declared Ekiti Governor Abayomi Oyebanji winner of Saturday's gubernatorial vote, the first back-to-back re-election in the state since 1999. Premium Times reports Oyebanji of the APC carried every local government area, leaving the opposition without a single LGA. Vanguard logged widespread vote-buying allegations, with PDP's Bejide accusing APC of distributing cash. IReV uploads stalled with 66 polling-unit results outstanding. President Tinubu congratulated Oyebanji. Watch the opposition's threatened legal challenges and INEC's transparency record ahead of 2027.
2
Rwanda and South Africa are taking concrete steps to rebuild ties after more than a decade of estrangement. AllAfrica reports high-level engagements between Kigali and Pretoria aimed at restoring a relationship long marked by suspicion and political tension. The thaw matters given South Africa's troop presence in eastern DRC and Pretoria's prior friction with Kigali over M23. Watch whether the diplomatic warming translates into coordinated positions on the Great Lakes peace track.
3
Niger and Benin held high-level talks in Cotonou over the weekend to normalize relations and reopen their shared border. Africanews reports the discussions focused on resolving a crisis that has kept the frontier shut since Niger's 2023 coup and the regional rupture that followed. Reopening would restore a key trade and transit corridor for landlocked, AES-member Niger. Watch for a concrete reopening timeline and whether the détente extends to the wider AES-ECOWAS standoff.
