Morning Memo
1
Burkina Faso cut diplomatic ties with France, hardening the Sahel's break with Paris. Captain Ibrahim Traoré's junta announced late Friday it severed relations with the former colonial power, accusing Paris of persistently acting against its interests, RFI reported. France called the move "hostile and baseless." The rupture extends the AES bloc's sovereignty-assertion line and removes another Western diplomatic channel from the central Sahel. Watch whether Mali and Niger follow with parallel downgrades and how Paris recalibrates regional posture.
2
Washington imposed a second tranche of sanctions on Sudan, barring Sudanese airlines. The State and Treasury departments announced measures "against those responsible for perpetuating violence" and fuelling the war, Dabanga reported. The aviation restrictions fall under the Chemical and Biological Weapons Control Act, whose second phase took effect June 26. Sudan's UN envoy refuted chemical-weapons allegations as unsubstantiated. The Sudanese Centre for Civil Aviation Studies said the curbs are unlikely to bite operationally but could complicate finance and commerce. Watch SAF-Washington friction after Khartoum disputed adviser Massad Boulos.
3
South Africa's June 30 anti-migrant deadline triggered multi-country evacuations. Uganda, Nigeria and Malawi moved to repatriate nationals as vigilante-led protests loomed, per Nile Post, Vanguard and Nyasa Times. President Ramaphosa warned that criminal conduct will meet "the full force of the law"; police cancelled all leave before the shutdown. GroundUp reported thousands of Zimbabweans and Malawians stranded at Beitbridge and repatriation sites. Watch whether the deadline triggers violence and the diplomatic fallout across SADC and East Africa.
