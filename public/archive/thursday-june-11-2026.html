Kenya's debt service consumed more than Sh1.35 trillion in the first nine months of FY2025/26, per the Controller of Budget. The JKIA award deepens Beijing's infrastructure footprint in East Africa's busiest hub at a moment of fiscal strain in Nairobi.

NIGERIA / AFRICOM
US-Nigeria Joint Strikes Claim 200+ Militants Killed in Northeast
Nigeria's Defence Headquarters and AFRICOM jointly announced that combined operations killed more than 200 Boko Haram and Islamic State West Africa Province fighters and destroyed key infrastructure in the northeast. Nigeria invited US forces, AFRICOM's Heyse confirmed. The DHQ figure is unverified by independent sources; body-count claims in this theater have historically been disputed. Watch for ISWAP reconstitution attempts through the Cameroon border corridor, which ACLED identifies as an active hidden conflict axis.

KENYA / CHINA
Chinese State Firm Wins $2.9B JKIA Upgrade After Adani Exit
A Chinese state-backed construction company secured a $2.9 billion (Sh375 billion) contract to modernize and expand Jomo Kenyatta International Airport, according to Bloomberg as reported by Capital FM. The award follows the collapse of the Adani Group deal and arrives as Kenya presents its tightest budget in years, with debt servicing exceeding Sh1.35 trillion in nine months. Opposition's United Alternative Government coalition released a rival "People's Budget" accusing Ruto's government of economic mismanagement. The JKIA deal consolidates Chinese infrastructure presence in East Africa's primary air hub.
Capital FM · Jun 11, 2026 ↗
MOZAMBIQUE
Islamic State Mozambique Attacks Displace More Than 15,000 in Cabo Delgado
Médecins Sans Frontières reported that repeated ISM attacks on Ancuabe district in May drove more than 15,000 people from their homes, compounding displacement already generated by years of insurgency in Cabo Delgado. MSF cited widespread fear of further violence as a multiplier on flight numbers beyond direct attack casualties. A separate international investigation published this week found that dozens of opposition members and journalists have been abducted or killed in Mozambique since the 2024 general elections, with figures from state security implicated (AllAfrica/Mozambique News Reports, Jun 11). The dual crisis—insurgency north, political violence south—reinforces Mozambique's fragility as LNG investment decisions loom.
MSF / AllAfrica · Jun 11, 2026 ↗
