Morning Memo
1
The UN Security Council warned that El Obeid could become "another El Fasher." Council members on June 21, 2026 expressed alarm at a substantial RSF troop build-up around the North Kordofan capital and urged the paramilitaries not to launch a ground assault, per UN News and Dabanga. RSF drone strikes killed more than 1,000 civilians between January and May, Sudan's SNA reported. AU Commission Chairperson Mahmoud Ali Youssouf and IGAD issued parallel concern statements. Watch for whether RSF converts encirclement into assault.
2
Nigeria's APC held Ekiti as Governor Biodun Oyebanji won a second term. INEC declared Oyebanji winner of Saturday's off-cycle governorship vote, making him the first Ekiti governor to secure consecutive re-election, Premium Times reported. The APC also took five of six bye-elections across Ondo, Enugu, Nasarawa, Kano and Kebbi, losing Rivers to the PDP, per Vanguard. ADC candidate Dare Bejide rejected the result; CDD observers flagged vote-buying and BVAS glitches. Watch 2027 positioning.
3
Xenophobic violence in KwaZulu-Natal is driving a Malawian exodus. The killing of a 29-year-old Malawian in Pietermaritzburg triggered an outflow of foreign nationals, Nyasa Times reported. South African government moved vulnerable Malawians to a Drive-Inn site as repatriation accelerated, per SAnews. Zulu King Misuzulu and Cape Town artists urged against attacks. Prophet Shepherd Bushiri claimed credit for buses the justice minister called unlawful. Watch regional diplomatic fallout.
