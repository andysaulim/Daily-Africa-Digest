Morning Memo
1
Xenophobic killings in South Africa have escalated into a state-to-state confrontation with Nigeria. Nigeria's Foreign Ministry condemned the deaths of two nationals — one allegedly killed by police after anti-migrant marches — and warned it could activate unspecified measures against Pretoria (This Day, Vanguard, July 6). South Africa rejected Abuja's compensation demand and told Nigeria to identify "drug dens" of its citizens (This Day). Uganda repatriated 560 nationals; a Zimbabwean was reportedly killed in Mpumalanga. Watch AfCFTA integration fallout and the July 10 final Nigerian evacuation flight.
2
Northern Nigeria's hunger crisis has reached its worst level in nearly a decade. The WFP says more than 17 million people across nine conflict-hit states face severe food insecurity, driven by violence and funding cuts (RFI via AllAfrica, July 6). The agency links the deterioration directly to insurgency, banditry and lost livelihoods. Nigeria's DHQ claims troops eliminated 1,597 criminals and rescued 1,516 hostages over six months (This Day). Watch donor shortfalls and displacement in Sokoto, Zamfara and Borno.
3
Tuareg rebels and Islamist fighters launched coordinated assaults on army bases and a prison across northern Mali. The army claimed it regained control after simultaneous attacks on several towns (DW via AllAfrica, July 6). ACLED separately tracks fighting for Anefis in the north. The assaults test the junta's Africa Corps-backed security posture and its FUAES alignment. Watch whether rebel coordination signals a sustained offensive along the Gao–Kidal axis.
