Morning Memo
1
RSF drone strikes killed at least 30 civilians across Sudan this week, with the deadliest hitting a funeral procession in El Obeid. The Emergency Lawyers Group and local sources tallied 23 civilian dead in El Obeid alone, plus additional strikes in Delling, Kadugli, and Sudri Locality (Dabanga, Jun 12). Rights groups attributed the attacks to the Rapid Support Forces. BBC confirmed the funeral procession targeting. The strikes underscore RSF willingness to hit civilian gatherings in North Kordofan, a critical front-line state. Watch for SAF counteroffensive signals and any Western condemnation specifics.
2
Nigeria evacuated 268 citizens from South Africa as xenophobic violence ripples across Southern Africa. Air Peace backed the operation, calling the situation 'deeply concerning' (Vanguard, Jun 12). The evacuees and Abuja rejected Pretoria's claim that all returnees were undocumented, blaming South Africa's Home Affairs delays for irregular status (Vanguard, Jun 12). Namibian 'order with me' traders halted South Africa trips over safety fears (New Era, Jun 12). The crisis is straining bilateral ties and disrupting cross-border commerce. Watch for additional African repatriation flights and AU statements.
3
The DRC's Ebola outbreak is expanding as WHO warns misinformation is undermining the containment response. The outbreak involves a rare Ebola species in a conflict-affected area, complicating health access (BBC, Jun 12). Authorities launched an online counter-disinformation campaign while the EU pledged €170 million for health and humanitarian response after Commissioner Hadja Lahbib's visit, warning 'we're sitting on a volcano' (RFI, Jun 12). Conflict dynamics in eastern DRC and population displacement magnify transmission risk. Watch for cross-border spread into the Great Lakes region.
